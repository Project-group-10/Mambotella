Sumaya Abdulrahman

I had just finished high school and I was waiting for my results. In the meantime I was just at home trying to make applications to several universities outside my home country. ALU was one of my very first applications. Once I received my results I submitted my application. Countless of essays I wrote, I did not think I would get in. A few months later I received a full ride scholarship to go to one of the most beautiful islands. It is said that heaven was copied after Mauritius. Indeed it was true..

They say a journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step. I believe that the true glory lies in the magic that happens before that; choosing to say yes to one self and having the zeal to step into the unknown. That pretty much summarizes my journey into tech. I have always been intrigued by technology and yearned to delve deeper into that space. However before taking that step, I had some doubts and uncertainties stemming from intrinsic and extrinsic factors.

I took a leap of faith regardless, and I'm beyond glad that I did because my single step exposed me to a world of opportunities and endless possibilities. This journey is a continuous learning and improvement of my journey despite the hurdles. ALU has given me the experience which has made the two years worthwhile. They said, you choose a mission not a major. You engage with a pan African community and join a great network for life. You learn through hands-on, real-world experiences and have a great group of peers. You create history as part of the leaders who transformed Africa. You get access to an affordable education, thus I got a full ride scholarship.

I got a part time study work experience with ALU. Working as a residential Advisor, I had learnt a lot of things. From organizing events to monitoring students and giving them the best campus life. ALU gave me the opportunity to meet and connect with people from all across the African continent in a way I would never have experienced elsewhere. The events held at school were very engaging and I got to learn different cultures. There is a lot of diversity amongst us students and everyone has something unique they offer.

People are more interested in what you build than in what you say. I love computers and technology in general because it almost incomprehensibly altered the human situation. For example, aircraft, robots, communication, and the list goes on. It is beautiful how studying computers and all that it entails can entangle itself into engineering, business, and even bring new technology into existence. All these bring me down to exactly my passions about computers, how to solve problems through programming, coding, and making elegant designs that can help people out there maneuver through their day-to-day activities. It is a beautiful and yet amazing experience when done right with a lot of passion.

This passion sparked when I was back in my village. I saw how girls were being forced to marry and they ended up dropping out of school because my community believes that girls are meant to stay at home and take care of the family. That is when I got an interest in helping my community to learn and engage in technological advancements as we are moving to a new era of technology. Educating young girls who are already married and cannot find a loophole to get out of it. This is my mission at ALU. You can read my recent resume here

Hallan Warigia Chege
